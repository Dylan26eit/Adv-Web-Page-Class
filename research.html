Copyright, Creative Common (CC) License, Fair Use

Copyright & CC Licenses

Basic Principles of Copyright:

Copyright is a form of intellectual property that gives creators the exclusive right to use and distribute their work. It applies automatically upon creation and protects original works such as literature, art, music, and more.

Types of Creative Commons (CC) Licenses:

  • Attribution (CC BY): Allows others to distribute, remix, and build upon the work, even commercially, as long as credit is given to the creator.
  • Attribution-ShareAlike (CC BY-SA): Similar to CC BY, but new creations must carry the same license as the original.
  • Attribution-NoDerivs (CC BY-ND): Allows for redistribution, commercial and non-commercial, as long as the work is unchanged and credit is given.
  • Attribution-NonCommercial (CC BY-NC): Allows others to remix, tweak, and build upon the work non-commercially, and although their new works must also acknowledge the creator, they don’t have to license their derivative works on the same terms.

Fair Use in New Zealand Law:

New Zealand's law provides limited allowances for "fair use" under copyright, mainly for purposes such as criticism, review, news reporting, and education. However, it is more restrictive compared to some other jurisdictions like the USA.

Privacy & Web Privacy Policy

Privacy & Privacy Policy

What is a Privacy Policy?

A privacy policy is a legal document that explains how an organization collects, uses, stores, and shares the personal data of its users or customers. It outlines users' rights regarding their personal information and ensures transparency regarding data handling practices.

New Zealand’s 13 Privacy Principles:

The 13 principles in New Zealand’s Privacy Act 2020 help regulate how personal information is handled by organizations. These principles ensure that individuals’ data is collected, used, and shared appropriately.

  • Principle 1: Only collect personal information for a lawful purpose.
  • Principle 2: Collect personal information directly from the individual concerned.
  • Principle 3: Ensure individuals are aware of why their information is being collected.
  • Principle 4: Collect personal information by lawful and fair means.
  • Principle 5: Ensure personal information is kept securely to prevent loss, misuse, or unauthorized access.
  • Principle 6: Give individuals access to their own personal information.
  • Principle 7: Allow individuals to correct any incorrect personal information.
  • Principle 8: Ensure personal information is accurate and up-to-date before using it.
  • Principle 9: Retain personal information only for as long as needed for the purposes for which it was collected.
  • Principle 10: Use personal information only for the purposes for which it was collected, unless consent is given for another use.
  • Principle 11: Do not disclose personal information unless authorized by the individual or by law.
  • Principle 12: Use unique identifiers for individuals only when it is necessary for the organization’s functions.
  • Principle 13: Ensure all necessary steps are taken to protect the information when transferring it to foreign entities or jurisdictions.

How to improve your website SEO?

Improving Website SEO

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is crucial to increasing the visibility of your website on search engines like Google. Here are five effective strategies to boost your SEO:

  • 1. Optimize Your Content: Ensure your content is relevant, high-quality, and includes targeted keywords. This will help search engines understand what your site is about and improve your rankings.
  • 2. Improve Site Speed: A faster website enhances user experience and helps with SEO. Make sure your website loads quickly on both desktop and mobile devices.
  • 3. Mobile-Friendliness: With the majority of searches being done on mobile devices, optimizing your website for mobile is essential. Make sure your site is responsive and user-friendly on all devices.
  • 4. Use Meta Tags and Descriptions: Include keyword-rich title tags and meta descriptions for each page. This helps search engines understand the content of your pages and improves click-through rates.
  • 5. Build Backlinks: High-quality backlinks from reputable websites can significantly boost your SEO. Focus on earning links from trustworthy sources to improve your site’s authority.

Factors you should know when choosing the right web hosting provider

Choosing the Right Web Hosting Provider

When selecting a web hosting provider, it's important to take several factors into account to ensure that your website will perform well. Here are five major factors to consider:

  • 1. Reliability and Uptime: Look for hosting providers with an uptime guarantee of at least 99.9%. Downtime can negatively impact user experience and SEO rankings.
  • 2. Speed and Performance: A hosting provider should offer fast servers with the capacity to handle high traffic. Slow performance can drive users away.
  • 3. Security: Ensure the provider offers robust security features such as SSL certificates, DDoS protection, and daily backups. This will protect your site from potential cyber threats.
  • 4. Customer Support: Choose a provider with responsive and knowledgeable customer support available 24/7. Fast and effective support can save you time during issues.
  • 5. Scalability: As your website grows, you may need more server resources. Select a hosting provider that can accommodate the scaling needs of your business.

Web performance and maintanence

Web Performance & Maintenance

5 Ways to Reduce Page Loading Time:

  • 1. Optimize Images: Use formats like WebP and compress images to reduce their size without losing quality.
  • 2. Minify CSS, JavaScript, and HTML: Remove unnecessary characters and spaces to make your code as lightweight as possible.
  • 3. Enable Browser Caching: Configure the browser to store parts of your site locally, reducing the need to re-download everything on return visits.
  • 4. Use a Content Delivery Network (CDN): Distribute your content across servers globally, ensuring faster loading times for users worldwide.
  • 5. Reduce HTTP Requests: Minimize the number of requests by combining files, using inline CSS, and reducing external scripts.

5 Important Website Maintenance Tasks:

  • 1. Backup Your Website Regularly: Create regular backups to ensure you have a recovery option in case of data loss or corruption.
  • 2. Update Software and Plugins: Keep your CMS, plugins, and themes updated to ensure compatibility and security.
  • 3. Check for Broken Links: Regularly scan your website for broken links and update or remove them to maintain a good user experience.
  • 4. Test Website Performance: Continuously monitor your website's speed and responsiveness to ensure it performs well under various conditions.
  • 5. Review Security Measures: Update passwords, review access control settings, and ensure that your security certificates are up to date.

Web Security

Common Cyber Attacks & Preventive Measures

5 Common Cyber Attacks:

  • 1. Phishing: A fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity via email or other communication channels.
  • 2. Ransomware: Malicious software that encrypts a victim’s data and demands a ransom for its release.
  • 3. SQL Injection: An attack that involves inserting malicious SQL queries into an input field to gain unauthorized access to a database.
  • 4. Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS): An attack that overwhelms a network or server with excessive traffic, causing it to become unavailable.
  • 5. Malware: Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems or data.

5 Preventive Measures:

  • 1. Educate Users: Provide training to employees and users about recognizing phishing attempts and safe online practices.
  • 2. Use Antivirus Software: Install and maintain updated antivirus software to detect and remove malware.
  • 3. Implement Firewalls: Use firewalls to filter incoming and outgoing traffic, protecting against unauthorized access.
  • 4. Regular Software Updates: Keep all software, including operating systems and applications, updated with the latest security patches.
  • 5. Backup Data: Regularly back up important data and store backups securely to minimize damage in case of ransomware attacks or data loss.
